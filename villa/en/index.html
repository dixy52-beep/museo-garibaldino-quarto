From the quiet of Villa Spinola to the glory of Marsala

Located in the Genoese district of Quarto, Villa Spinola (now home to the Garibaldian Museum of Genoa) was the crucial setting of the Risorgimento. After leaving the Parliament in Turin, with his heart set on the Sicilian enterprise, Giuseppe Garibaldi arrived in Genoa on April 15, 1860. He chose Villa Spinola as his home, staying as a guest of his friend and comrade-in-arms, Major Candido Augusto Vecchi. The villa's location was strategic: far enough from the center of Genoa to ensure privacy, yet close enough to the sea to be ideal for organizing the imminent departure.

A logistics hub for the revolution

For twenty days, the rooms of the villa were transformed into a feverish and secret command center. According to the chronicles of the time:

  • Military Logistics: 1,019 rifles and related ammunition were stockpiled to equip the volunteers.
  • War Chest: 8,000 lire were collected and managed as the initial fund for the expedition.
  • Secret Diplomacy: The villa was the meeting place between Garibaldi and Cavour's emissary, Giuseppe La Farina, to secure the tacit support of the Sardinian government.

Names and Faces within the Villa Walls

In these corridors, the destinies of the most active patriots crossed: Francesco Crispi, who brought encouraging news from Sicily; Nino Bixio, who received orders here to finalize preparations for the seizure of the steamers Piemonte and Lombardo; and Agostino Bertani, the organizational mind of the Genoese movement.

The Night of May 5th: Towards the Legend

The bold plan reached completion on the night between May 5th and 6th, 1860.

  • Hundreds of volunteers had gathered around the villa: from Bergamo, Emilia, Tuscany, Genoa, and many students from Pavia.
  • While Bixio went to the port of Genoa to seize, with a "simulated violence", the steamers of the Rubattino company (the Piemonte and the Lombardo), Garibaldi left Villa Spinola to reach the rock of Quarto.
  • At 9:00 AM on May 6th, the two steamers carrying about 1,100 volunteers set sail towards Marsala.

A Living Museum in the General's Rooms

The Halls of History

Visit the rooms of Villa Spinola where Garibaldi planned the Expedition. Walk where history was written and feel the emotion of those crucial moments.

Relics and Documents

The museum exhibits original documents of the expedition, evidence of the passage of the Thousand, weapons, and period manuscripts.

Heritage of Quarto

The villa is a national monument that continues to pass down the values of the Risorgimento.
